### **US_FL_LAB: Florida Labor Law**

**CSI Components**:
- **Citation Endogamy** (0.52): Florida courts cite primarily own precedent, right-to-work framework rigid.
- **Sacralization** (0.60): "Business-friendly" environment sacralized, labor protections critiqued as anti-growth.
- **Costly Signaling** (0.55): Labor law practice dominated by management-side firms, union lawyers rare.
- **Institutional Control** (0.62): Department of Economic Opportunity (DEO) favors employers, weak enforcement.

**REI Components**:
- **Implementation Rate** (0.42): Minimum wage amendment (2020, $15 by 2026) implemented but exemptions for tipped workers.
- **Outcome Alignment** (0.38): Goal was living wage; enforcement weak, wage theft common. Unemployment insurance among worst in US (COVID-19 revealed dysfunctional system).
- **Adaptability** (0.35): Minimal. DEO failures documented but unreformed.

**REI Total**: 0.383

**Key Reform**: Minimum Wage Amendment (2020)
**Sources**: National Employment Law Project Florida data, Economic Policy Institute state analysis
