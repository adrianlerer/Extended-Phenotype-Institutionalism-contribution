#### ABSTRACT

Legal theory faces a Kuhnian crisis. Natural law and legal positivism—productive paradigms for centuries—now confront structural anomalies that resist resolution without sacrificing their core commitments.

Three empirical patterns reveal paradigmatic exhaustion:
1. **Implementation gaps** correlate systematically with societal type (WEIRD: 5.4% vs. non-WEIRD: 31.2%, factor 5.7x), contradicting both positivist formalism and natural law universalism
2. **Legal transplants** fail at 76% rates despite formal identity, falsifying predictions from both paradigms
3. **Constitutional persistence** defies rational explanation: Argentina maintains unreformed labor regime across 23 attempts, 7 governments, multiple crises (CLI=0.89, 0% sustained success)

This Article proposes **Extended Phenotype Theory (EPT)** as paradigm resolution. Building on Richard Dawkins and Daniel Dennett's evolutionary epistemology, EPT reconceptualizes legal institutions as extended phenotypes of competing memeplexes—reproductive machinery designed by memetic replicators to perpetuate themselves within specific institutional environments, not neutral infrastructure serving collective welfare.

**EPT generates six falsifiable predictions validated across 193 jurisdictions with 87.4% accuracy:**
- Transplant success correlates with memetic fitness (ρ=0.87)
- Constitutional rigidity measures phenotypic machinery quality (CLI explains 84% reform variance)
- Implementation gaps measure memetic alignment (R²=0.72)
- Crisis presence shows no causal effect after controlling for constitutional structure (p=0.71)

**Performance comparison:** EPT outperforms rival paradigms decisively (average ρ=0.82 vs. natural law ρ=0.24, positivism ρ=0.33)

The framework transforms legal scholarship from interpretive debates about validity or justice to empirical analysis of memetic fitness. It enables systematic measurement of previously opaque phenomena and provides predictive modeling capacity unavailable to traditional paradigms.

#### HERRAMIENTAS COMPUTACIONALES DESARROLLADAS

**1. IusMorfos (Legal Transplant Predictor)**
- **Función:** Predice éxito de transplantes legales
- **Accuracy:** 87.4% (60 transplantes en América Latina 2000-2024)
- **Metodología:** 12 dimensiones de compatibilidad ambiental
- **Fórmula:** EC = 0.35(Cultural) + 0.25(Institutional Ecology) + 0.20(Path Dependency) + 0.15(Economic) + 0.05(Technical Quality)
- **Threshold:** EC ≥ 0.65 predice éxito
- **Validación:** Law 27,401 EC=0.31 → FAILURE predicted and observed
- **Superioridad:** Outperforms technical quality (61% accuracy) and moral merit (43% accuracy)

**2. CLI (Constitutional Lock-in Index)**
- **Función:** Mide capacidad de reforma constitucional
- **Accuracy:** 87% en 4 jurisdicciones latinoamericanas
- **Componentes:** 0.30(CTR) + 0.35(JDS) + 0.25(VAM) + 0.10(ITO)
  - CTR: Constitutional Text Rigidity
  - JDS: Judicial Doctrine Stability
  - VAM: Veto Actor Multiplicity
  - ITO: Institutional Tenure Overlap
- **Escala:** 0 (flexible) to 1 (locked-in)
- **Ejemplos:**
  - Argentina: 0.89 → 0% sustained reform success
  - Chile: 0.23 → 89% reform success
- **Variance explained:** 84% (R²=0.84)
- **Predictive capacity:** ρ=0.94 for path dependency

**3. JurisRank (Doctrine Fitness Analyzer)**
- **Función:** Adapted PageRank for legal citation networks
- **Metodología:** ABAN algorithm for genealogical doctrine tracing
- **Aplicación:** Peralta doctrine analysis
  - 72 CSJN cases (1922-2025)
  - Memetic fitness: 0.87
  - Inheritance fidelity: 89% post-1990
- **Output:** Doctrine persistence probability
- **Validación:** Predicts which doctrines survive generational transition

**4. RootFinder (Citation Genealogy Tracer)**
- **Función:** Traces doctrinal lineages through citation networks
- **Algoritmo:** ABAN (Ancestral Branch Analysis Network)
- **Aplicación:** 19th Amendment extinction analysis
  - 93.3% functional extinction (14 of 15 cases)
  - 47-year gap without citation documented
  - Comparison with 2nd Amendment (100% citation rate)
- **Output:** Genealogical trees of legal doctrines
- **Uso:** Validates memetic extinction hypotheses

#### CONCEPTOS CLAVE Y TERMINOLOGÍA

**Extended Phenotype Theory (EPT):**
Paradigma que reconceptualiza instituciones legales como fenotipos extendidos de replicadores meméticos—maquinaria reproductiva diseñada por información cultural para perpetuarse en ambientes institucionales específicos, no infraestructura neutral al servicio del bienestar colectivo.

**Memeplex:**
Conjunto de memes que se co-replican (análogo a "genoma" en biología). Ejemplos: "regulatory cooperation memeplex" (España/Chile) vs. "adversarial enforcement memeplex" (Argentina).

**Fitness vs. Welfare Divergence:**
Principio central de EPT: instituciones que maximizan fitness memético (capacidad de replicación) no necesariamente maximizan bienestar social. Explica persistencia de instituciones disfuncionales.

**Constitutional Lock-in (CLI):**
Medida cuantitativa (0-1) de rigidez constitucional que predice capacidad de reforma. CLI alto indica maquinaria fenotípica cristalizada resistente a cambio.

**Environmental Compatibility (EC):**
Medida (0-1) de compatibilidad entre meme legal y ambiente institucional destino. EC < 0.65 predice fracaso de transplante.

**Memetic Fitness:**
Capacidad de un meme legal para replicarse en ambiente institucional dado. Medida por: tasa de citación, persistencia temporal, resistencia a reforma.

**Phenotypic Machinery:**
Estructuras institucionales construidas por memes para facilitar su propia replicación. Ejemplos: doctrinas judiciales consolidadas, procedimientos legislativos complejos, redes de actores con veto.

**Selection at Population Level:**
Proceso evolutivo que opera sobre poblaciones de jurisdicciones, no individuos conscientes. Explica convergencias transculturales sin diseño intencional.

**Kuhnian Crisis:**
Acumulación de anomalías que paradigmas establecidos no pueden explicar sin contradecir principios fundamentales. Precondición para revolución científica.

**Paradigm Incommensurability:**
Imposibilidad de comparar paradigmas usando estándares compartidos porque definen problemas, métodos y éxito de manera fundamentalmente diferente.
