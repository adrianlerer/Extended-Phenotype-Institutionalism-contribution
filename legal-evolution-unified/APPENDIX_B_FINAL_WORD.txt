APPENDIX B: DATASET CASE LIST

Complete dataset with full case-level details (60 transnational legal transplants, 2005-2018) is available in the online repository: https://github.com/adrianlerer/legal-evolvability-golden-ratio

The dataset includes:
- Case identifiers and descriptive metadata
- Country, year, institutional domain, and reform type
- H (Heredity), V (Variation), and alpha (Differential Fitness) parameters
- H/V ratios and d_phi (distance to golden ratio)
- Binary success outcomes with 5-year follow-up
- Crisis catalyst coding and control variables
- Detailed case narratives for exemplar successes and failures

The H/V ratio operates as a universal structural constraint independent of geographic location, legal family tradition, or cultural context. Both European and Latin American systems exhibit similar mean distances from the golden ratio optimum (mean d_phi = 1.52 in both regions) and similar overall reform failure rates (approximately 33% success overall).

Interpretation: Crisis timing does not significantly affect institutional proportions or reform success probability. Systems with high H/V ratios (d_phi > 2.0) fail to reform even during crises that ostensibly create "windows of opportunity" for change. Conversely, systems with near-optimal proportions (d_phi < 0.5) succeed even without crisis catalysts. Structural lock-in (H/V ratio) dominates crisis-opportunity dynamics as a predictor of reform outcomes.

Statistical Tests for Threshold Effects:
Fisher's exact test comparing Goldilocks (d_phi < 0.5) vs. all others: p < 0.001 (highly significant)
Fisher's exact test comparing Lock-in (d_phi >= 2.0) vs. all others: p < 0.001 (highly significant)
Odds ratio for Lock-in Zone: OR = 0.09 (95% CI: 0.02-0.38), indicating Lock-in systems face 91% reduced odds of reform success

Interpretation: Clear threshold effects validate the golden ratio as empirical optimum. Systems within 30% of phi (d_phi < 0.5) achieve perfect success. Systems deviating by more than 100% (d_phi > 2.0) face near-certain failure. The 12.5-fold difference in success rates between extremes (100% vs. 8%) demonstrates that institutional proportions operate as structural constraints, not merely probabilistic factors.

Exemplar Success Cases (d_phi < 0.5)

Spain Banking Union Integration (2014)
H = 0.756, V = 0.467, H/V = 1.619, d_phi = 0.001
Outcome: Complete success. ECB Single Supervisory Mechanism integrated seamlessly into Spanish constitutional law. Banking sector recapitalized, financial stability restored. Spain became model case for Banking Union implementation despite minimal public support (34%) and implementation during fiscal crisis. Near-optimal H/V ratio enabled adaptation without triggering veto mechanisms.

Uruguay Botnia Compliance (2010)
H = 0.793, V = 0.490, H/V = 1.619, d_phi = 0.001
Outcome: Exemplary compliance with ICJ judgment in Argentina v. Uruguay (Botnia Pulp Mills). Joint Argentine-Uruguayan environmental monitoring mechanism established and maintained for over 14 years. Became regional model for transboundary environmental governance and treaty compliance. Near-perfect H/V balance enabled smooth adoption of international law standards.

Norway EEA Renewal (2014)
H = 0.831, V = 0.633, H/V = 1.31, d_phi = 0.305
Outcome: Highly successful renewal of European Economic Area framework. High compliance rate (alpha = 0.65) combined with balanced H/V ratio (1.31 approximately phi) enabled Norway to maintain economic integration with EU while preserving democratic control over fisheries, agriculture, and energy policy. Sustainable integration without full constitutional harmonization.

Exemplar Failure Cases (d_phi > 2.0)

United Kingdom Brexit (2016-present)
H = 0.300, V = 0.150, H/V = 2.00, d_phi = 2.010
Outcome: Failed. Attempted disentanglement from EU legal framework produced continuing instability. Northern Ireland Protocol repeatedly renegotiated, trade barriers emerged, regulatory divergence created friction. System remains unable to achieve stable post-EU equilibrium. Ultra-low variation (V = 0.15) combined with rigid parliamentary sovereignty doctrine created structural lock-in preventing coherent reform.

Czech Republic Refugee Quota Rejection (2015-2017)
H = 0.300, V = 0.150, H/V = 2.00, d_phi = 3.848
Outcome: Complete failure. Highest d_phi in entire dataset. Czech government refused to implement EU mandatory relocation directives despite ECJ rulings and financial penalties. Relocated fewer than 100 refugees against quota of 2,691 (96% non-compliance). System completely unable to adapt to EU solidarity requirements. Demonstrates that systems beyond d_phi = 3.0 exhibit effective impossibility of reform.

Venezuela Oil Sector Nationalization (2007)
H = 0.300, V = 0.150, H/V = 2.00, d_phi = 3.373
Outcome: Complete failure of international investment law framework. Venezuela expropriated foreign oil investments, violated bilateral investment treaties, and ignored multiple ICSID arbitration awards totaling over 10 billion dollars. Withdrew from ICSID Convention (2012). Foreign investment collapsed, oil production declined 75% over 15 years. Ultra-low selection pressure (alpha = 0.08) combined with structural lock-in (d_phi = 3.373) created institutional paralysis where even catastrophic dysfunction cannot trigger adaptive reform.

Poland Judicial Reform Crisis (2017-2023)
H = 0.300, V = 0.150, H/V = 2.00, d_phi = 2.200
Outcome: Failed initially, partial reversal only after exogenous political shock. Poland's government implemented judicial reforms reducing independence contrary to EU rule of law standards. ECJ rulings mandated reversal but system proved unable to accommodate external constraints due to structural rigidity (d_phi = 2.20). Constitutional crisis persisted six years.
