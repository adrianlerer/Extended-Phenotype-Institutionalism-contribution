- Ley 14.250 (1953) - Collective Bargaining Law
- Constitución Nacional Art. 14bis (1957 reform)
- Ley 23.546 (1988) - Procedural Law

**Statistical Sources**:
- INDEC (Instituto Nacional de Estadística y Censos) - Labor statistics 2023
  - URL: https://www.indec.gob.ar/
  - Wage data, employment figures
- ILO (International Labour Organization) - Argentina Country Profile
  - URL: https://www.ilo.org/global/lang--en/index.htm
  - Union density, collective bargaining coverage

**Academic Sources**:
- Lerer (2024) SSRN 5402461 - CLI empirical analysis
- Torre, Juan Carlos & Pastoriza, Elisa (2002) - "La democratización del bienestar"

**Empirical Codification**:
- Rent capture: 3.2% of wage mass (union dues + obra social contributions)

- Cost bearers: 12M formal workers (INDEC employment data)
- Veto players: CGT (national union), provincial unions, political parties

## Missing Data & Limitations

### Beneficiary Counts
- Some counts are estimates based on:
  - OSHA/labor ministry employment data
  - Union membership statistics
  - Regulatory compliance staffing projections

### Rent Capture Percentages
- Calculated from:
  - Official budgetary data where available
  - Academic estimates of compliance costs
  - Industry association reports

### Subjective Components (0-1 scales)
- "Survival necessity" and "founding document mentions" coded qualitatively
- Inter-rater reliability not established (single coder)
- Future work: Multiple expert coding with Cohen's kappa validation
