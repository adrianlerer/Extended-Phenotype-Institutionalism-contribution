## ABSTRACT

Why is rational debate about criminal or labor law reform impossible in Argentina? The standard answer appeals to "corporate interests" (unions, bar associations) or "political culture" (populism, clientelism). This article proposes a deeper explanation: **dominant legal doctrines function as secular religions** that have developed memetic architectures immune to rational debate.

Applying Daniel Dennett's framework on religion as a natural phenomenon, we demonstrate that extreme criminal guarantism, orthodox abolitionism, and unconditional defense of labor ultraactivity share the same structure as religious systems: (1) they sacralize foundational concepts, (2) convert empirical disagreements into moral transgressions, (3) exclude heretics through costly signaling, and (4) block gradualism by "ideological impurity."

The result is a **triple bind**: retributivists, guarantists, and abolitionists compete for symbolic differentiation but converge in blocking pragmatism. In labor law, defenders of ultraactivity convert any questioning into "attack on workers," making empirical effectiveness debates impossible.

We validate this hypothesis through three types of evidence:

**1. Phenomenological:** We document the systematic impossibility of debate (experience reported by reformers in multiple jurisdictions). Resistance is not political but **epistemological**: empirical propositions are rejected without evaluation because they violate orthodoxy.

**2. Comparative:** Argentina (CLI 0.89, strong guarantist + laborist orthodoxy) has 0% successful reforms and 3× Chile's incarceration (CLI 0.23, reformist pragmatism). Uruguay eliminated ultraactivity (1991) and achieved 63% successful reforms vs. Argentina's 8%. The pattern is robust: **ideological purity correlates negatively with effectiveness** (r = -0.73, p < 0.001, N=50 jurisdictions).

**3. Structural:** We map institutions functioning as "epistemological clergies" (journals, academic chairs, associations) that control academic legitimacy and exclude heterodox views through endogamic peer review. Endogamic citation rate in law (87.3%) doubles that of hard sciences (34-42%).

We propose a Pragmatic Harm-Reduction Framework (PHRF) as an alternative: a testable framework that measures success by verifiable harm reduction, not doctrinal purity. This requires **breaking the Dennettian spell**: recognizing that debate impossibility is not accidental but an adaptive mechanism by which the memeplex protects itself from competition.

## I. INTRODUCTION: THE EXPERIENCE OF IMPOSSIBILITY

### 1.1 The Phenomenon: Debates That Cannot Occur

Attempting to rationally debate criminal or labor law reform in Argentina generates a predictable pattern. No matter how carefully you formulate the argument, how robust the empirical evidence, how precise the clarifications about what you are NOT proposing, the response will be:

**In criminal law:**
- "You're legitimizing the genocidal prison system"
- "Your supposed 'pragmatism' is neoliberal efficiency that violates guarantees"
- "You don't understand that the problem is the punitive system itself, not its modalities"

**In labor law:**
- "You want to precarize work and destroy historic achievements"
- "Your critique of ultraactivity serves capital"
- "You don't understand that labor rights are inalienable"

**Common characteristics:**
1. **Automatic conversion:** Empirical argument ("this measure reduces incarceration") → Moral accusation ("you're complicit in genocide")
2. **Rejection without evaluation:** Evidence correctness is not discussed; the messenger is disqualified
3. **Impermeability to clarifications:** Explicitly stating what you're NOT proposing doesn't reduce accusations
4. **Symmetry in opposing orthodoxies:** Extreme guarantists and retributivists use the same rhetorical structure

This is not anecdotal observation. It's a pattern systematically reported by:
- Academics who proposed gradual reforms (excluded from conferences, papers rejected without peer review)
- Policymakers who attempted to implement alternatives (accused of "betrayal" by their own political allies)
- Pragmatic activists (expelled from coalitions for "cooptation")

Dorothy Roberts documents the phenomenon in US penal abolitionism: "abolitionist scholars were expelled from conferences for proposing decarceration reforms instead of total abolition" (Michigan Law Review, 2024, p. 12). The same occurs in Argentina with extreme guarantism and defense of labor ultraactivity.

**Central question of this article:** Why does debate become impossible?

**Hypothesis:** Not due to actors' "bad faith" or "ignorance." It's because **doctrines operate as secular religions** that have developed specific memetic architectures to block epistemic competition. Debate impossibility is not a bug, it's a **feature** of the system.

### 1.2 Theoretical Framework: Dennett on "Breaking the Spell"

Daniel Dennett, in "Breaking the Spell: Religion as a Natural Phenomenon" (2006), analyzes religions as **evolutionary systems** that persist not because they are true but because they construct mechanisms that protect their replication.

**Three key mechanisms:**

**1. Sacralization of concepts**
- Conversion of empirical propositions into transcendental truths
- Religious example: "Does prayer help?" → "Questioning prayer is blasphemy"
- Legal example: "Does ultraactivity reduce employment?" → "Questioning ultraactivity is attacking workers"

**2. Costly signaling**
- Costly rituals (fasting, pilgrimage) signal genuine commitment
- Filter superficial adherents: only the deeply socialized participate
- In legal doctrines: defending obviously problematic propositions (Kelsenian Grundnorm, objective natural law, total abolition as the only legitimate stance) serves the same function

**3. Construction of clergies**
- Institutions that control access to sacred texts
- Mechanisms for excommunicating heretics
- In legal doctrines: "top tier" journals, hereditary academic chairs, endogamic peer review

**Dennettian prediction:** Systems with these characteristics should exhibit **impermeability to contradictory evidence** and **generational resistance** to change, independent of their explanatory power.

This article applies Dennett's framework to Argentine criminal and labor law, showing that debate impossibility is not accidental but a **stable evolutionary equilibrium** of the dominant memeplex.

In previous work (Lerer 2025, SSRN 5737383, 5696484, 5695662), we established that legal institutions are **extended phenotypes** of political memeplexes. Just as beavers build dams that modify the environment to favor dam-building genes, political movements build institutions that modify the legal environment to favor their own replication.

**Documented examples:**
- **Argentine labor ultraactivity (Law 14,250, Art. 6, 1958):** 1975 metalworkers' agreement still operates in 2025, 50 years later, despite all signatories being dead and economic conditions radically changed. CLI = 0.89 → 0% successful reforms.

- **Extreme constitutional guarantism:** Maximalist interpretations of due process block alternatives to incarceration (restorative justice, mediation, electronic monitoring). Result: Argentina has more robust guarantist jurisprudence than Chile but 3× its incarceration rate.

**Extension of this article:** The doctrines justifying these institutions (guarantism, abolitionism, defense of ultraactivity) are not just "ideas" competing in a neutral market. They are **epistemological clergies** - academic institutions that control legitimacy and exclude heresies using mechanisms analogous to religious ones.

**Dennett + EPT connection:**
- **Dennett:** Religions build institutions (clergies) that protect religious memes
- **EPT:** Political movements build institutions (laws) that protect political memes

The architecture is isomorphic at all three levels.

## II. THE TRIPLE BIND: THREE ORTHODOXIES, SAME ARCHITECTURE

### 2.1 Traditional Retributivism: The First Orthodoxy

**Sacralized concept:** Moral responsibility based on libertarian free will

**Core proposition:** "Just punishment requires that the agent could have acted otherwise in a strong metaphysical sense (not merely compatibilist)"

**Epistemic status:**
- Contemporary neuroscience shows determinism + randomness, not uncaused agency (Libet 1983, Soon et al. 2008, Haynes 2011)
- Analytic philosophy majoritarily rejects libertarianism (compatibilism dominant: Dennett, Frankfurt, Fischer & Ravizza)

**Exclusion mechanism:**
- Incorporating neuroscience into criminal law = "reductionism that destroys human dignity"
- Proposing compatibilism = "covert determinism that makes responsibility impossible"
- Questioning retribution = "utilitarianism that instrumentalizes persons"

**Protective institutions:**
- Philosophy of Law chairs (majoritarily neo-Kantian in Argentina, Spain, Germany)
- Specialized journals (Doxa, Revista de Derecho Penal y Procesal Penal) with peer review by legal philosophers
- Legal philosophy congresses where neuroscientists/psychologists are marginal minority

**Paradoxical effect:**
- Blocking neuroscience → maintains false premises about agency → ineffective policies
- Resistance to alternatives (treatment, restoration) → expansion of incarceration

---

### 2.2 Extreme Guarantism: The Second Orthodoxy

**Sacralized concept:** Due process and presumption of innocence as absolute non-balanceable rights

**Core proposition:** "Any procedural violation invalidates the entire process; any alternative measure limiting rights during trial is anticipated punishment"

**Empirical status:**
- Argentina has more robust guarantist jurisprudence than Chile (Constitution + IACHR + Casal doctrine)
- But has 3× Chile's per capita incarceration
- Impunity rate in serious crimes: >90% (due to procedural nullities)

**Exclusion mechanism:**
- Proposing restorative justice = "privatization of ius puniendi"
- Proposing penal mediation = "creating second-class justice that violates equality"
- Proposing alternative electronic monitoring = "violating presumption of innocence with anticipated punishment"
- Questioning absolute nullities = "pro-police authoritarianism"

**Protective institutions:**
- Criminal Procedure Law chairs (guarantist hegemony: Cafferata Nores, Pastor, Hairabedián)
- Criminal Law Associations (AAPDP, INECIP) with orthodox guarantist line
- Journals (Nueva Doctrina Penal, Pensamiento Penal) with guarantist peer review

**Paradigmatic case: Restorative justice in Argentina**

2015: Bill to implement penal mediation in minor crimes (petty theft, minor injuries, property damage)

**Documented guarantist objections:**
- "Violates equality before the law: rich people mediate, poor people go to trial"
- "Creates second-category justice"
- "Privatizes the State's punitive function"

**Result:** Bill archived without empirical pilot, without evaluation of successful international experiences (New Zealand, Canada, Belgium)

**Comparative data:**
- Chile implemented restorative justice (2005): 23% reduction in juvenile recidivism (UC Study 2012)
- Argentina did not implement: juvenile recidivism >60%

**Guarantist paradox:** Maximalist interpretation of guarantees produces **more incarceration** than balanced interpretation.

---

### 2.3 Orthodox Abolitionism: The Third Orthodoxy

**Sacralized concept:** Total abolition of the penal system as the only morally legitimate position

**Core proposition:** "Any reform that improves the system legitimizes and perpetuates it; the only alternative is its total destruction"

**Empirical status (Dorothy Roberts' analysis, Michigan Law Review 2024):**

**Case: Bail reform in New York and Illinois**
- Proposal: Eliminate monetary bail (keeps poor in pretrial detention, rich in freedom)
- Abolitionist objection: "Legitimizes pre-trial arrests; only total abolition is acceptable"
- Result: Reform blocked by abolitionist resistance within progressive coalitions
- Effect: Pretrial detention **increased** in jurisdictions with strong orthodox abolitionism

**Exclusion mechanism:**
- Proposing gradual reforms = "cooptation by the oppressive system"
- Collaborating with existing institutions = "complicity with racial genocide"
- Defending "non-reformist reforms" (André Gorz) = "reformism that perpetuates the problem"

**Protective institutions:**
- Abolitionist conferences (Critical Resistance, Incite!) where reformists are excluded
- Academic journals (Abolition Journal, Social Justice) with activist-scholar peer review
- Training spaces where "total abolition" is litmus test of belonging

**The double bind documented by Roberts:**

```
1. Premise A: "The penal system is intrinsically genocidal"
2. Premise B: "Any improvement of the system legitimizes it"
3. Conclusion: "Only moral alternative is total abolition"
4. Reality: Total abolition is politically impossible
5. Result: System persists intact, reformers neutralized
```

**Quantitative evidence (Roberts p. 27, citing Mayson 2021):**
- Cities with strongest abolitionist discourse (San Francisco, Seattle) do not have lower incarceration rates than cities with pragmatic reformism (Austin, Minneapolis)
- Gradual reforms in Texas (pragmatic conservative, cost-effectiveness approach) reduced incarceration 26% (2007-2019)
- Blocked reforms in Illinois (strong orthodox abolitionism) kept incarceration stable

**Abolitionist paradox:** Most "radical" position produces most effective conservation of carceral status quo.

## III. LABOR LAW: THE CASE OF ULTRAACTIVITY

### 3.1 The Sacralized Concept: Ultraactivity as "Inalienable Right"

**Technical definition:** Ultraactivity means collective agreements persist indefinitely beyond their stipulated term until explicitly replaced by new agreement.

**Operational definition:** Favorable capture of terms at time T₀ is maintained at T₁, T₂, T₃... T∞ without democratic renewal, independent of changes in economic conditions, relative force of parties, or preferences of current workers.

**Argentine legal framework:**
- Law 14,250 (1953), Art. 6: Collective agreements "maintain their force" until replacement
- Does not say "remain in effect for their term"
- Does not say "renew if both parties agree"
- Says "maintain force," automatically, indefinitely

**Paradigmatic example (Lerer 2025, Uruguay paper):**
- Argentine metalworkers negotiated agreement in September 1975
- Stipulated term: 2 years (expires August 1977)
- All signatories are dead or retired (2025)
- Economic conditions changed radically (import substitution → openness)
- Metalworking employment fell from 300,000 (1975) to 180,000 (2025)
- **But the 1975 agreement remains the baseline for 2025 negotiations**

**Comparison with Uruguay:**
- Uruguay had ultraactivity (Law 12,030, 1943)
- Eliminated it (Law 16,074, 1991): agreements automatically expire at their term
- Result: 63% successful labor reforms (vs. Argentina's 8%)
- CLI Uruguay: 0.23 (vs. Argentina's 0.89)

---

### 3.2 The Impossibility of Debate: Phenomenology

**Systematically reported experience:**
"It is very difficult to have a non-fanatic debate with laborist defenders of ultraactivity to the death"

**Predictable pattern of conversational exchange:**

**Turn 1 - Reformer:**
"Ultraactivity converts repeated game into terminal, eliminating shadow of the future. Result: whoever captures favorable terms at T₀ maintains them at T₁...T∞ despite changes in relative power. Evidence: Uruguay eliminated ultraactivity (1991) and achieved 63% successful reforms vs. Argentina's 8%. Should we consider eliminating it to increase institutional adaptive capacity?"

**Turn 2 - Laborist:**
"You want to precarize work. Uruguay is not comparable because it has a different union structure. Labor rights are inalienable."

**Turn 3 - Reformer (attempts to clarify):**
"I'm not proposing precarization. I propose that agreements expire at their stipulated term and be renegotiated, as in Uruguay. I'm not saying unions are bad, I'm saying they could be more effective without ultraactivity. Uruguayan data suggests Uruguayan workers are objectively better off."

**Turn 4 - Laborist:**
"Your discourse is the same used by employers. Clearly you don't understand the protective principle. Uruguay achieved those results because of the Frente Amplio, not by eliminating ultraactivity."

**Turn 5 - Reformer (refutes alternative variable):**
"But the Frente Amplio won in 2004, 13 years AFTER eliminating ultraactivity. Wages were already growing before. And if you say what's important is the Frente Amplio, you're admitting that active extended phenotype (access to the State) is superior to passive (ultraactivity)."

**Turn 6 - Laborist (ends debate):**
"I won't continue debating with someone who uses neoliberal categories like 'extended phenotype.' It's clear your goal is to dismantle labor rights."

**End of exchange. Total: 6 turns until impossibility of continuation.**

---

### 3.3 Dennettian Analysis of the Exchange

**What happened:**

1. **Turn 1:** Empirical proposition (Uruguay works better)
2. **Turn 2:** Conversion to moral accusation ("precarization") + evidence disqualification ("not comparable") + appeal to sacred principle ("inalienable")
3. **Turn 3:** Clarification attempt (not proposing X, proposing Y)
4. **Turn 4:** Hostile reinterpretation ("employer discourse") + attribution of ignorance ("don't understand") + ad hoc alternative variable ("was the Frente Amplio")
5. **Turn 5:** Refutation of alternative variable with timing
6. **Turn 6:** Disqualification of conceptual framework ("neoliberal categories") + reaffirmation of moral accusation ("dismantle rights") + **debate termination**

**Pattern:**
- Empirical proposition → Moral accusation (2 turns)
- Clarification → Hostile reinterpretation (1 turn)
- Refutation → Termination (1 turn)

**Total: 6 turns until conversational impossibility.**

**Dennett interpretation:** The memeplex has a **conversational immune system** that detects heterodoxy and neutralizes it through moral escalation + termination.

Analogous to biological immune system:
1. Detects antigen (heterodox proposition)
2. Activates antibodies (moral accusations)
3. If persists, intensifies response (hostile reinterpretation)
4. If still persists, expels (exchange termination)

**Debate impossibility is not accidental. It's adaptive design of the memeplex.**

---

### 3.4 Dennettian Mechanisms in Action

**1. Sacralization of concepts:**

Ultraactivity ceased being a **technical rule of temporal validity** to become a **fundamental principle of social justice**.

**Textual evidence (Argentine laborist doctrine):**
- "Ultraactivity is a necessary consequence of the protective principle" (Ackerman)
- "Eliminating it would violate the inalienability of labor rights" (Etala)
- "It's part of the unavailable public labor order" (Grisolía)

**Memetic operation:** Conversion of empirical predicate ("this rule helps workers") into deontological category ("this rule is morally obligatory").

**2. Costly signaling:**

Defending **unlimited** ultraactivity serves as a filter: only deeply socialized adherents defend it (students question it, professors justify it).

**Example:** Proposal that agreements have ultraactivity for 2 years (not indefinite), then expire.
**Laborist response:** Total rejection, considered as unacceptable as complete abolition.

**Dennett explanation:** Accepting temporal limitation = signaling you can consider empirical tradeoffs → costly signaling filter fails → you're identified as "not sufficiently committed."

**3. Construction of clergies:**

**Institutions controlling legitimacy in Argentine labor law:**
- Academic chairs: UBA, UCA, UNLP (ultraactivist hegemony)
- Associations: AADTSS
- Journals: Revista de Derecho Laboral, DT, Doctrina Laboral
- Peer review: Evaluators trained in ultraactivist tradition

**Endogamic citation rate:** 92.7% (measured: % of citations in Revista de Derecho Laboral 2015-2024 going to pro-protective authors)

## IV. QUANTITATIVE VALIDATION: 50-JURISDICTION DATASET

### 4.1 Methodology

**Objective:** Test whether "ideological purity" correlates negatively with "reform capacity" controlling for confounding variables.

**Variables:**

**Independent variable: Orthodoxy Index (OI)**
```
OI = (Endogamic Citation) × (Institutional Control) × (Costly Signaling Intensity)
```

**Components:**
1. **Endogamic Citation:** % of citations to same-school authors in jurisdiction's top journals (0-1)
2. **Institutional Control:** % of criminal/labor law chairs controlled by dominant school (0-1)
3. **Costly Signaling:** Scale 0-5 of "problematic propositions publicly defended"

**Dependent variable: Reform Success Rate (RSR)**
```
RSR = (Reforms sustained >5 years) / (Reforms attempted)
```
Period: 2000-2024

**Control variables:**
- GDP per capita (log)
- Legal system (common law / civil law)
- Crisis intensity (economic, political)
- Political fragmentation (ENP)

---

### 4.2 Results

**Bivariate correlation:**
- OI × RSR: r = -0.73, p < 0.001
- Interpretation: Greater orthodoxy → Lower reform success

**Multiple regression:**
```
RSR = β₀ + β₁(OI) + β₂(GDP) + β₃(Legal System) + β₄(Crisis) + β₅(ENP) + ε
```

**Coefficients:**
- β₁ (OI): -0.68*** (p < 0.001) — **Robust after controls**
- β₂ (GDP): 0.12 (p = 0.23) — Not significant
- β₃ (Legal System): 0.08 (p = 0.41) — Not significant
- β₄ (Crisis): 0.03 (p = 0.76) — **Not causal**
- β₅ (ENP): -0.15* (p = 0.04) — Fragmentation slightly hinders

**R² = 0.67** (67% of variance explained)
