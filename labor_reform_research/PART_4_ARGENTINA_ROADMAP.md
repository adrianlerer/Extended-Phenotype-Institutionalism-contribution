### YEAR 8 (2033): SEVERANCE PAY REFORM (Marginal)

#### Q1 2033: "Ley de Actualizaci√≥n de Indemnizaciones Laborales"

**Context**: If procedural reforms successful + enhanced UI in place, attempt modest severance reduction

**Current System**:
- 1 month salary per year of service (no cap)
- Typical worker (10 years): 10 months severance
- Cost to employer: HIGH

**Proposed Reform** (VERY MODEST):

| Worker Category | Current Severance | Proposed Severance | Compensation | Net Effect |
|-----------------|-------------------|-------------------|--------------|------------|
| **New hires only** (hired after 2034) | 1 month/year | **0.75 months/year** (max 10 months) | Enhanced UI (70% wage for 12 months) | Slight reduction for employers |
| **Existing workers** (hired before 2034) | 1 month/year | **NO CHANGE** (grandfathered) | N/A | No impact |

**Key Safeguards**:
- ‚úÖ Existing workers fully protected (no regression for current workforce)
- ‚úÖ UI enhanced BEFORE severance reduced (safety net in place)
- ‚úÖ Cap at 10 months (similar to international standards)
- ‚úÖ Only applies to NEW hires (forward-looking only)

**Framing**:
> "This is not a reduction of rights but a MODERNIZATION of the social protection system. New workers will have STRONGER unemployment insurance (12 months at 70% wage) in exchange for slightly more flexible severance. Current workers keep all existing rights. This is the European 'flexicurity' model adapted to Argentina."

**Constitutional Analysis**:

**Pro-Constitutionality Arguments**:
1. Art. 14bis protects existing workers ‚Üí They are grandfathered
2. New workers have CHOICE (accept job with new terms or seek elsewhere)
3. Net protection arguably HIGHER (UI > severance in many cases)
4. International precedent (Brazil 2017, Spain 2012 did similar)

**Anti-Constitutionality Arguments**:
1. Creates two-tier labor market (discrimination)
2. "Condiciones dignas" (Art. 14bis) applies to ALL workers
3. Future workers cannot waive constitutional rights
4. Slippery slope toward full deregulation

**CSJN Probability**: 35-40% upholds (higher than ultraactivity because grandfathering + compensation)
