## üáÆüá≥ INDIA (3 cases)

### **IN_CRIM: India Criminal Law**

**CSI Components**:
- **Citation Endogamy** (0.55): Supreme Court of India extensive self-citation, colonial-era precedents still influential.
- **Sacralization** (0.60): Indian Penal Code (1860) treated with near-reverence despite outdated provisions (e.g., Section 377).
- **Costly Signaling** (0.58): Senior Counsel designation, legal education stratified (National Law Schools vs others).
- **Institutional Control** (0.62): Supreme Court exercises broad discretion via PIL (Public Interest Litigation), but reforms often blocked by lower courts.

**CSI Total**: 0.588

**REI Components**:
- **Implementation Rate** (0.42): Criminal Law (Amendment) Act 2013 (post-Nirbhaya) partially implemented, many Fast Track Courts delayed.
- **Outcome Alignment** (0.38): Goal was reducing violence against women; conviction rates remain low (~27%), pendency high (30M+ cases).
- **Adaptability** (0.35): Limited adjustment despite data showing reforms ineffective. Police training insufficient.

**REI Total**: 0.383

**Key Reform**: Criminal Law (Amendment) Act 2013 (Nirbhaya reforms)
**Sources**: Mitra (2016) "Indian Criminal Justice System", National Crime Records Bureau data 2020

### **IN_LAB: India Labor Law**

**CSI Components**:
- **Citation Endogamy** (0.68): Labor law extremely complex (44 central laws + state laws), precedent-heavy, insular.
- **Sacralization** (0.72): Industrial Disputes Act (1947) treated as protecting workers, resistant to flexibility reforms.
- **Costly Signaling** (0.65): Labor law practice requires navigating maze of regulations, signals expertise.
- **Institutional Control** (0.70): Multiple overlapping authorities (Labour Ministry, state governments, tribunals), gatekeeping fragmented.

**CSI Total**: 0.688

**REI Components**:
- **Implementation Rate** (0.28): Labor Code reforms (2019-2020) consolidating 29 laws into 4 codes - implementation stalled, only 2 states adopted.
- **Outcome Alignment** (0.25): Goal was formalization of labor market; informality remains 90%+ of workforce.
- **Adaptability** (0.22): Minimal adjustment. Reforms blocked by unions, political opposition. Firms stay small to avoid regulations.

**REI Total**: 0.250

**Key Reform**: Labour Code consolidation (2019-2020)
**Sources**: World Bank Doing Business 2020, Kapoor (2019) "Understanding India's Labour Market"

### **IN_CONST: India Constitutional Law**

**CSI Components**:
- **Citation Endogamy** (0.48): Supreme Court cites extensively from Commonwealth, US precedent, more open than other domains.
- **Sacralization** (0.52): "Basic Structure Doctrine" (Kesavananda Bharati, 1973) treated as untouchable, limits amendability.
- **Costly Signaling** (0.50): Constitutional litigation expensive, dominated by elite lawyers.
- **Institutional Control** (0.54): Supreme Court collegium system for appointments, self-perpetuating elite.

**CSI Total**: 0.510

**REI Components**:
- **Implementation Rate** (0.55): Right to Education Act (2009) implemented widely but quality issues persist.
- **Outcome Alignment** (0.48): Goal was universal education; enrollment increased (95%+) but learning outcomes poor.
- **Adaptability** (0.45): Some adjustment (mid-day meals, infrastructure), but teacher quality unchanged.

**REI Total**: 0.493

**Key Reform**: Right to Education Act (2009)
**Sources**: Khosla (2020) "India's Founding Moment", ASER Annual Report 2020
