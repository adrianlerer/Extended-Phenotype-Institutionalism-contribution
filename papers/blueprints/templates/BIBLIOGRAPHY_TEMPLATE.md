# Bibliography Template: Chicago Author-Date Format

**Version**: 1.0  
**Last updated**: 2025-11-20  
**Style**: Chicago Manual of Style (17th ed.) - Author-Date System

---

## Structure

This template provides **two sections**:
1. **Academic References** (books, articles, working papers)
2. **Jurisprudential References** (case law, Fallos)

---

## I. Academic References

### A. Books

**Format**: Author Last, First. Year. *Title in Italics*. City: Publisher.

**Examples**:

Ackerman, Bruce. 2004. *The Emergency Constitution*. Yale Law Journal 113 (5): 1029-1091.

Dawkins, Richard. 1982. *The Extended Phenotype: The Long Reach of the Gene*. Oxford: Oxford University Press.

Gargarella, Roberto. 2013. *Latin American Constitutionalism, 1810-2010: The Engine Room of the Constitution*. Oxford: Oxford University Press.

Nino, Carlos Santiago. 1992. *Fundamentos de Derecho Constitucional: Análisis Filosófico, Jurídico y Politológico de la Práctica Constitucional*. Buenos Aires: Astrea.

---

### B. Journal Articles

**Format**: Author Last, First. Year. "Article Title in Quotes." *Journal Name in Italics* Volume (Issue): Page range.

**Examples**:

Helmke, Gretchen. 2004. "Enduring Uncertainty: Court-Executive Relations in Argentina During the 1990s and Beyond." In *Argentine Democracy: The Politics of Institutional Weakness*, edited by Steven Levitsky and María Victoria Murillo, 139-162. University Park: Pennsylvania State University Press.

Maynard Smith, John. 1973. "The Logic of Animal Conflict." *Nature* 246 (5427): 15-18.

Rubin, Jared. 2017. "Rulers, Religion, and Riches: Why the West Got Rich and the Middle East Did Not." *Cambridge University Press*.

---

### C. Book Chapters

**Format**: Author Last, First. Year. "Chapter Title in Quotes." In *Book Title in Italics*, edited by Editor First Last, Page range. City: Publisher.

**Examples**:

Miller, Jonathan M. 2000. "Evaluating the Argentine Supreme Court Under Presidents Alfonsín and Menem (1983-1999)." *Southwestern Journal of Law and Trade in the Americas* 7: 369-433.

Sen, Amartya. 1999. "Democracy as a Universal Value." *Journal of Democracy* 10 (3): 3-17.

---

### D. Working Papers / Unpublished

**Format**: Author Last, First. Year. "Title in Quotes." Paper type (if applicable). Institution. URL or "Unpublished manuscript."

**Examples**:

Negretto, Gabriel L. 2012. "Making Constitutions: Presidents, Parties, and Institutional Choice in Latin America." Working Paper. CIDE, Mexico City.

[Your Name]. 2025. "General Welfare and Common Good as Evolutionary Stable Strategies: An Extended Phenotype Analysis of Argentine Constitutional Law (1922-2025)." Unpublished manuscript. SSRN. [INSERT URL WHEN POSTED]

---

### E. Online Sources / Datasets

**Format**: Organization or Author. Year. "Title in Quotes." Dataset name (if applicable). URL. Access date (if no publication date).

**Examples**:

V-Dem Institute. 2023. "Democracy Report 2023: Defiance in the Face of Autocratization." V-Dem Institute, University of Gothenburg. https://v-dem.net/documents/29/V-dem_democracyreport2023_lowres.pdf

World Bank. 2024. "World Development Indicators." https://databank.worldbank.org/source/world-development-indicators. Accessed November 15, 2024.

---

## II. Jurisprudential References (Argentine Case Law)

### A. CSJN Fallos (Supreme Court Decisions)

**Format**: *Case Name*, Fallos Tomo:Página (Date). [Brief description if first mention]

**Examples**:

*Peralta, Luis Arcenio y otro c/ Estado Nacional (Ministerio de Economía - Banco Central)*, Fallos 313:1513 (December 27, 1990). [Expanded emergency powers to economic measures]

*Barra, Rodolfo Carlos c/ Poder Ejecutivo Nacional*, Fallos 314:1738 (November 7, 1991). [Defined "common good" as "the good of the State organized as such"]

*ALITT S.A. c/ Secretaría de la Competencia, Desregulación y Defensa del Consumidor*, Fallos 329:3093 (July 11, 2006). [Reversed Barra doctrine, adopted capability-based framework]

*CHA, Asociación Civil c/ Ministerio de Relaciones Exteriores*, Fallos 315:1492 (July 7, 1992). [First LGBT rights case, cited "common good" to deny marriage]

---

### B. Lower Court Decisions (If Cited)

**Format**: *Case Name*, Court, Docket Number (Date). [Source if not widely published]

**Example**:

*Freyre, María Rachid y otra c/ Registro Nacional de las Personas*, Tribunal de Familia de Buenos Aires, Expediente 15957/2009 (November 10, 2009). [First same-sex marriage in Argentina, provincial level]

---

### C. International Cases (If Comparative)

**Format**: *Case Name*, Court, Citation (Date). [Jurisdiction]

**Examples**:

*Dobbs v. Jackson Women's Health Organization*, 597 U.S. ___ (June 24, 2022). [U.S. Supreme Court overruled Roe v. Wade]

*Reference re Secession of Quebec*, [1998] 2 S.C.R. 217 (August 20, 1998). [Canadian Supreme Court on constitutional principles]

---

## III. Special Cases & Notes

### A. Multiple Authors (3+ authors)

**First mention**: List all authors  
**Subsequent mentions**: First author et al.

**Example**:
- First: Levitsky, Steven, María Victoria Murillo, and Juan Carlos Torre. 2015.
- Later: Levitsky et al. 2015.

---

### B. Same Author, Multiple Works in Same Year

Add lowercase letters: (Author 2020a), (Author 2020b)

**Example**:
- Gargarella, Roberto. 2013a. *Latin American Constitutionalism*.
- Gargarella, Roberto. 2013b. "The Legal Foundations of Inequality."

---

### C. No Author (Institutional Authorship)

Use organization name as author.

**Example**:
- V-Dem Institute. 2023. "Democracy Report 2023."

---

### D. Archival Sources (If Used)

**Format**: Archive Name, Box #, Folder #, Document Name (Date).

**Example**:
- Archivo General de la Nación (Argentina), Caja 45, Legajo 1203, "Dictamen del Procurador General sobre bien común" (March 15, 1943).

---

## IV. Verification Checklist

Before finalizing bibliography:

- [ ] All in-text citations have corresponding bibliography entry
- [ ] All bibliography entries are cited at least once in text
- [ ] Fallos citations verified in SAIJ database (existence + date)
- [ ] URLs tested (all links work, no 404 errors)
- [ ] Alphabetical order by author last name
- [ ] Consistent formatting (italics, punctuation, spacing)
- [ ] Chicago 17th edition compliance (use Zotero or Endnote style)

---

## V. Common Errors to Avoid

| **Error** | **Correct** | **Example** |
|-----------|-------------|-------------|
| Missing Fallos date | Always include date | Fallos 313:1513 (December 27, 1990) ✅ |
| Wrong citation format | "Name, Fallos X:Y" not "Fallos X:Y, Name" | *Peralta*, Fallos 313:1513 ✅ |
| URL without access date | Include if no pub date | Accessed November 15, 2024 ✅ |
| Inconsistent capitalization | Follow Chicago rules | "The Extended Phenotype" not "The extended phenotype" ✅ |
| Missing italics for case names | Always italicize | *Dobbs v. Jackson* ✅ not Dobbs v. Jackson ❌ |

## VI. Reusability Instructions

### For New Papers

1. **Copy this file** to your paper directory:
   ```bash
   cp papers/blueprints/templates/BIBLIOGRAPHY_TEMPLATE.md papers/2026_NewPaper/
   ```

2. **Replace example entries** with your actual sources

3. **Preserve section structure**:
   - Section I: Academic References
   - Section II: Jurisprudential References

4. **Add new categories** if needed (e.g., "Government Reports", "Newspapers")

5. **Run verification checklist** before submission
