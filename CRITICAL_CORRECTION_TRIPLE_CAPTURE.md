## Theoretical Foundation

### 1. Memetic-Cultural Capture (Dennett Pure)

**Definition**: Diffuse, unorganized adhesion to cultural norms that block reform

**Characteristics**:
- **No conspiratorial agent** (not conspiracy theory)
- Meme self-replicates through socialization, education, media
- Carriers genuinely believe in norm's sacredness
- Operates through System 1 cognitive heuristics (Kahneman)

**Argentina Example**:
- **Meme**: "The State must protect workers because employers have more power"
- **Historical origin**: Peronism 1946-1955 (constitutionalization of social rights)
- **Memetic replication**:
  - Education system: "1949 Constitution = social conquest"
  - Media: Labor conflict coverage biased pro-worker
  - Family: Parents teach children "never trust the boss"

**Evidence**:
- Latinobar√≥metro 2023 survey:
  - 78% Argentines: "State must guarantee dignified employment"
  - 65% Chileans (low CLI): "Market should regulate employment"
- Difference is NOT corporate, it's **cultural-cognitive**

**Dennett Mechanism**:
- No "union conspiracy" to impose this meme
- Meme self-perpetuates because:
  1. Activation of protectionist heuristic (System 1)
  2. Reinforcement rituals (May 1st marches, Worker's Day)
  3. Shielding: Questioning = "heartless neoliberal"

---

### 2. Corporate Capture (Organized Guardians)

**Definition**: Interest groups exploit cultural meme for sectorial benefit

**Characteristics**:
- **Identifiable actors** (CGT, UIA, professional associations)
- Direct economic interest in maintaining status quo
- Strategic use of cultural meme for legitimation

**Argentina Example**:
- CGT didn't invent protectionist meme, but **instrumentalizes it**:
  - Blocks 2017 labor reform (Macri) using "human rights" rhetoric
  - Dennett mechanism: **Professional guardianship**
    - Union leaders = clergy of labor meme
    - Their power/income depends on system complexity
    - Perverse incentive: Simplification = existential threat

**EPT Evidence**:
- Argentina: 23 structural labor reform attempts (1991-2024) ‚Üí **0 successes**
- Uruguay (CLI 0.34): 8 attempts ‚Üí **5 successes** (63%)
- Difference: In Uruguay, unions are weaker (less guardianship)

**Critical Point**: CGT is symptom, not root cause
- If CGT didn't exist, cultural meme would still block reforms
- Proof: In 1990s, Menem negotiates with CGT ‚Üí achieves minor reforms
- In 2000s, Kirchner doesn't need to negotiate with CGT (cultural climate hostile to reform)

---

### 3. Oligarchic Capture (Institutional Control)

**Definition**: Elites control courts and use ultraactivity to perpetuate status quo

**Characteristics**:
- **Actors**: Supreme Court judges, specialized labor tribunals
- **Mechanism**: Political appointments ‚Üí structural ideological bias
- **Strategy**: Invoke "social constitutional clause" to annul reforms

**Argentina Example**:
- Judicial CLI Component = 0.32 (25% of total CLI)
- 2003-2015: Kirchner appoints progressive judges
  - Supreme Court systematically rules pro-union
  - High JurisRank: Courts cite jurisprudence in closed network
- 2017: Macri attempts labor reform
  - CGT calls general strike (corporate capture)
  - Courts declare unconstitutional via "progressivity of rights" (oligarchic capture)
  - Public opinion majority supports strike (memetic-cultural capture)

**Interaction with other layers**:
- Judges don't invent ultra-protectionist doctrine from nothing
- They exploit preexisting cultural meme for legitimation
- But, once institutionalized, judicial doctrine has its own life:
  - New judges (even conservative) must follow precedents
  - Ultraactivity = extended phenotype of constitutional meme

## Theoretical Implications

### 1. Resolves Apparent Paradoxes

**Paradox 1**: Why can weak presidents sometimes reform, but strong presidents fail?
- **Answer**: If CLI_corporate and CLI_oligarchic are low, even weak president can succeed (Chile 1990s)
- If CLI_memetic is high, even dictator fails (Pinochet couldn't eliminate labor code entirely)

**Paradox 2**: Why do economic crises sometimes enable reform, sometimes not?
- **Answer**: Crisis can lower CLI_corporate (unions weakened) and CLI_oligarchic (court legitimacy questioned)
- But CLI_memetic has high inertia ‚Üí if cultural norms don't shift, lock-in returns post-crisis

### 2. Connects to Adjacent Literatures

**Veto Players Theory (Tsebelis)**:
- CLI_corporate maps to institutional veto players
- CLI_oligarchic maps to judicial veto players
- **Innovation**: CLI_memetic adds cultural veto players (not in Tsebelis)

**Historical Institutionalism (Mahoney & Thelen)**:
- CLI_memetic = "ideational path dependence"
- CLI_corporate = "power distribution lock-in"
- CLI_oligarchic = "institutional reproduction mechanisms"

**Cultural Cognition (Kahan)**:
- CLI_memetic operationalizes "cultural worldview constraints"
- Shows how System 1 heuristics create institutional stickiness
