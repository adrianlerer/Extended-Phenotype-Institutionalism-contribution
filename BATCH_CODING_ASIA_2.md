### **ID_CRIM: Indonesia Criminal Law**

**CSI Components**:
- **Citation Endogamy** (0.62): Indonesian courts heavily cite domestic precedent, Dutch colonial law influence persists.
- **Sacralization** (0.68): Islamic law (Sharia) integration in Aceh province treated as untouchable. Anti-blasphemy laws sacred.
- **Costly Signaling** (0.60): Legal profession fragmented but Jakarta elite dominates constitutional cases.
- **Institutional Control** (0.65): Corruption in judiciary well-documented (Corruption Perception Index 37/100), gatekeeping by political-legal networks.

**CSI Total**: 0.638

**REI Components**:
- **Implementation Rate** (0.35): Anti-corruption reforms (KPK reforms 2019) weakened after implementation, enforcement selective.
- **Outcome Alignment** (0.32): Goal was reduce corruption; KPK powers curtailed, high-profile cases stalled.
- **Adaptability** (0.28): Minimal adjustment. Protests against reforms ignored.

**REI Total**: 0.317

**Key Reform**: KPK (Anti-Corruption Commission) Law revision (2019)
**Sources**: Aspinall & Mietzner (2019) "Problems of Democratisation in Indonesia", Transparency International 2020

### **ID_LAB: Indonesia Labor Law**

**CSI Components**:
- **Citation Endogamy** (0.58): Labor courts reference domestic precedent, Dutch labor code legacy strong.
- **Sacralization** (0.62): "Worker protection" rhetoric sacralized despite informal economy dominance (60%+).
- **Costly Signaling** (0.56): Labor law practice dominated by Jakarta firms, regional disparity high.
- **Institutional Control** (0.60): Ministry of Manpower controls regulations, coordination with APINDO (employers) and unions.

**CSI Total**: 0.590

**REI Components**:
- **Implementation Rate** (0.38): Omnibus Law (Job Creation Law, 2020) implemented despite massive protests, enforcement patchy.
- **Outcome Alignment** (0.35): Goal was investment and formalization; informal sector remains 60%, worker protections weakened.
- **Adaptability** (0.32): Limited responsiveness to labor concerns. Constitutional Court struck down some provisions (2021).

**REI Total**: 0.350

**Key Reform**: Omnibus Law (Job Creation Law, 2020)
**Sources**: ILO Indonesia Report 2021, World Bank Indonesia Economic Quarterly

---

### **ID_CONST: Indonesia Constitutional Law**

**CSI Components**:
- **Citation Endogamy** (0.52): Constitutional Court somewhat more open, but still domestic-focused.
- **Sacralization** (0.55): Pancasila (state philosophy) treated as sacred, limits reform scope.
- **Costly Signaling** (0.50): Constitutional litigation accessible via NGOs, but elite lawyers dominate.
- **Institutional Control** (0.54): Constitutional Court relatively independent post-1998 reforms, but recent politicization concerns.

**CSI Total**: 0.528

**REI Components**:
- **Implementation Rate** (0.52): Direct regional elections (Pilkada) maintained despite centralization attempts.
- **Outcome Alignment** (0.48): Goal was democratic accountability; results mixed (some dynastic politics).
- **Adaptability** (0.45): Moderate responsiveness. Court checks executive overreach occasionally.

**REI Total**: 0.483

**Key Reform**: Regional Autonomy reforms (2014-2020)
**Sources**: Mietzner (2020) "Authoritarian Innovations in Indonesia"

## üáµüá≠ PHILIPPINES (3 cases)

### **PH_CRIM: Philippines Criminal Law**

**CSI Components**:
- **Citation Endogamy** (0.50): Supreme Court of Philippines cites US precedent extensively (common law tradition), more open than neighbors.
- **Sacralization** (0.55): "War on Drugs" rhetoric under Duterte (2016-2022) became sacralized, evidence-based critique suppressed.
- **Costly Signaling** (0.52): Legal profession accessible but elite Manila lawyers dominate constitutional cases.
- **Institutional Control** (0.58): Executive influence on judiciary strong during Duterte presidency, independence concerns.

**CSI Total**: 0.538

**REI Components**:
- **Implementation Rate** (0.38): Criminal justice reforms (2018-2020) partially implemented, but "war on drugs" dominated.
- **Outcome Alignment** (0.35): Goal was reduce crime; extrajudicial killings 12,000-30,000 (estimates vary), rule of law weakened.
- **Adaptability** (0.32): Limited responsiveness to human rights concerns. ICC investigation ongoing.

**REI Total**: 0.350

**Key Reform**: Criminal justice reforms vs War on Drugs (2016-2022)
**Sources**: Human Rights Watch Philippines reports, Curato (2017) "Politics of Anxiety, Politics of Hope"

---

### **PH_LAB: Philippines Labor Law**

**CSI Components**:
- **Citation Endogamy** (0.48): Labor courts reference domestic and some ILO standards, moderately open.
- **Sacralization** (0.52): "Endo" (end of contract) practice criticized but treated as economic necessity by employers.
- **Costly Signaling** (0.50): Labor law practice accessible, unions active but fragmented.
- **Institutional Control** (0.54): Department of Labor and Employment (DOLE) gatekeeps, coordination with business groups strong.

**CSI Total**: 0.510

**REI Components**:
- **Implementation Rate** (0.48): Security of Tenure Act (2019) vetoed by Duterte, alternative DO 174 implemented weakly.
- **Outcome Alignment** (0.45): Goal was end contractualization; "endo" persists, enforcement weak.
- **Adaptability** (0.42): Moderate adjustment. Some regional enforcement improvements.

**REI Total**: 0.450

**Key Reform**: Security of Tenure / Anti-Endo reforms (2018-2019)
**Sources**: ILO Philippines Decent Work Programme, Philippine Statistics Authority Labor Force Survey

---

### **PH_CONST: Philippines Constitutional Law**

**CSI Components**:
- **Citation Endogamy** (0.42): Supreme Court cites US precedent extensively, comparative law engaged.
- **Sacralization** (0.45): 1987 Constitution post-Marcos transition valued, but not rigidly sacred.
- **Costly Signaling** (0.48): Constitutional litigation accessible via civil society, less elite-dominated.
- **Institutional Control** (0.50): Supreme Court maintains some independence despite executive pressure.

**CSI Total**: 0.463

**REI Components**:
- **Implementation Rate** (0.58): Impeachment attempts blocked, but Court stood firm on some issues (e.g., Sereno ouster questioned).
- **Outcome Alignment** (0.52): Goal was constitutional accountability; mixed results, some checks on executive maintained.
- **Adaptability** (0.50): Moderate responsiveness. Court struck down some Duterte policies (e.g., drug war aspects).

**REI Total**: 0.533

**Key Reform**: Constitutional checks during Duterte presidency (2016-2022)
**Sources**: Gatmaytan (2020) "The Illusion of Intangibility: The Philippine Supreme Court and Judicial Independence"
