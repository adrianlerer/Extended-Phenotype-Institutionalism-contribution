### **IL_CRIM: Israel Criminal Law**

**CSI Components**:
- **Citation Endogamy** (0.45): Israeli Supreme Court cites comparative law extensively (US, UK, Germany), moderately open.
- **Sacralization** (0.52): Security laws treated as untouchable given existential threats narrative. Administrative detention beyond evidence-based scrutiny.
- **Costly Signaling** (0.48): Legal profession highly competitive, Hebrew University/Tel Aviv University dominance.
- **Institutional Control** (0.50): Attorney General powerful, but Supreme Court maintains independence via judicial review (no written constitution framework).

**CSI Total**: 0.488

**REI Components**:
- **Implementation Rate** (0.58): Criminal procedure reforms (2018) implemented, public defender expansion successful.
- **Outcome Alignment** (0.52): Goal was access to justice; conviction rates remain high (~90%) but procedural fairness improved.
- **Adaptability** (0.50): Moderate adjustment. Security considerations limit scope of criminal justice reform.

**REI Total**: 0.533

**Key Reform**: Criminal Procedure Reform (2018)
**Sources**: Barzilai (2003) "Communities and Law", Israel Ministry of Justice data

### **IL_LAB: Israel Labor Law**

**CSI Components**:
- **Citation Endogamy** (0.42): Labor courts engage with ILO standards, comparative law (especially German codetermination).
- **Sacralization** (0.48): Histadrut (labor federation) legacy sacralized despite declining membership (20% of workforce).
- **Costly Signaling** (0.45): Labor law practice accessible, but Tel Aviv dominance.
- **Institutional Control** (0.50): Ministry of Economy gatekeeps, coordination with Histadrut and Manufacturers Association.

**CSI Total**: 0.463

**REI Components**:
- **Implementation Rate** (0.62): Minimum wage increases (2015-2020) implemented, enforcement strong.
- **Outcome Alignment** (0.58): Goal was reduce working poverty; poverty rate declined 15% (2015-2019).
- **Adaptability** (0.55): Responsive to data. Adjustments made based on Central Bureau of Statistics monitoring.

**REI Total**: 0.583

**Key Reform**: Minimum Wage Policy (2015-2020)
**Sources**: Kristal & Cohen (2017) "Decentralization of Collective Agreements", OECD Israel data

### **IL_CONST: Israel Constitutional Law**

**CSI Components**:
- **Citation Endogamy** (0.38): Supreme Court highly comparative (US, Canada, Germany, South Africa precedents).
- **Sacralization** (0.42): Basic Laws treated as quasi-constitutional but amendable, less rigid than written constitutions.
- **Costly Signaling** (0.40): Constitutional litigation accessible via NGOs (ACRI, Adalah), relatively open.
- **Institutional Control** (0.45): Supreme Court powerful but recent political pressure (judicial reform controversy 2023).

**CSI Total**: 0.413

**REI Components**:
- **Implementation Rate** (0.65): Basic Law: Human Dignity and Liberty (1992) applied consistently, judicial review active.
- **Outcome Alignment** (0.60): Goal was rights protection; mixed results (strong for Jewish citizens, weaker for Arab citizens).
- **Adaptability** (0.58): Responsive to evolving norms. LGBT rights expanded, though Orthodox influence limits some areas.

**REI Total**: 0.610

**Key Reform**: Basic Laws framework (ongoing application since 1992)
**Sources**: Hirschl (2004) "Towards Juristocracy", Barak (2006) "The Judge in a Democracy"
